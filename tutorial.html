Welcome to the Tutorial

The aim of the game is to try to identify whether or not the emails are fake/phishing or real/safe.
When trying to identify the email, there are a number of different types of identifiers. Some of these are:
Email having a generic type of greeting, The name of the sender and the email address not matching up,
The sender trying to create a sense of urgency so that the reader doesn’t notice something in the email, Bad grammar and fake links.
If you suspect that the email is a fake email, then you will click on the button that says “Smells fishy” However, if you believe it to be a real email, click on the “A-OK!” button.
This will then display a pop-up menu which will, in turn, show either a well-done message or an incorrect message depending on if you answered correctly.

The annotated email shows that there are a couple of ways to spot a malicious email. The first shown is a generic greeting, which in this instance is “Hello.” This could be a sign that they don’t know anything about you and don’t want to offend anyone.
The second one is bad spelling. This could indicate that the email is coming from an untrustworthy source because the email could be rushed and not checked over—unlike a legitimate email where someone would read over what they have written.
The third issue with the email is that when the mouse is over the link, it shows that the link is not to a Google site and leads to a different website.
When navigating the game, there will be a nav bar on the left which will act like the email client. This will have the header of the email. However wehn you close the pop-up box it will direct you to the next page as well.
Once you have finished with the email you are on, all you will have to do is click on one of the emails on the left to open the next email.
